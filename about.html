How We Got Here

Jason’s Journey — Where It All Started

In 2021, Tonya’s nephew Jason decided his story didn’t need to be written anymore. That loss was devastating — it changed what “normal” means for the whole family, forever. It made Tonya start thinking about her own past, and even her own son. We have both been where Jason was. That dark cloud is very hard to crawl out from underneath.

The stigma that society puts on those of us who “are not okay” makes it even harder to reach out for help. Usually it’s easy to comfort someone else and talk them through devastating situations — but when it was time to help her own family, Tonya found it was one of the hardest things she had ever done. She reached out blindly, not knowing where to turn, and found the American Foundation for Suicide Prevention.

Since August 2021, Tonya and her daughter have become advocates for AFSP, NAMI, and Overdose Lifeline.

In 2021, with the help of her daughter, Tonya founded “Jason’s Journey” — to keep his story going and to help raise awareness for others who are suffering. They quickly realized it wasn’t just Jason who had a twizted journey. Everyone has a journey, and every journey is twizted in its own way. So began Twizted Journeys.

Nothing is too taboo to discuss here and you will not face judgment. Suicide and mental health are usually associated with pain, darkness, and despair. It doesn’t have to be — let’s use peace, love, and maybe even some amusement as we help one another remember: it’s okay not to be okay.

Peer Support Group

Finding Forward

Finding Forward is a free, peer-led support group meeting every Tuesday evening in Shelbyville, Indiana. It’s a safe, non-judgmental space for people carrying the weight of suicide loss and mental health struggles.

📅
Every Tuesday Evening
Shelbyville, Indiana
👤
Adults 18+
All genders, identities, and backgrounds welcome
😘
Always Free
No fees, no membership, no barriers

Who Finding Forward is for:

  • ✓ Anyone who has lost a loved one to suicide
  • ✓ Individuals coping with grief, trauma, or emotional stress
  • ✓ People supporting a friend or family member with mental health struggles
  • ✓ Community members seeking coping skills, connection, and resources
  • ✓ Individuals with disabilities or sensory needs — inclusive and accessible

Our Mission

What We Do & Who We Serve

Twizted Journeys hosts S.A.F.E. (Suicide Awareness For Everyone) events, fundraisers, workshops, and support groups for mental health awareness and suicide prevention. We also strive to bring unity, comfort, and acceptance to suicide loss survivors.

Our events allow people to share the journey of loved ones who have cut their story short, and their own journey of continuing after a pause. We believe every program should be completely free — no membership, no cost, ever.
