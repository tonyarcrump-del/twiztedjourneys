Welcome!

Nothing is too taboo to discuss here and you will not face judgment.

Suicide and Mental Health are usually associated with pain, darkness and despair. It doesn't have to be. Let's try a new approach and use peace, love and maybe even some amusement as we help one another, remind one another and even ourselves that ; it's ok not to be ok ;

We host S.A.F.E. (Suicide Awareness for Everyone) events, fundraisers, workshops and support groups for mental health awareness and suicide prevention. We also strive to bring unity, comfort and acceptance to suicide loss survivors.

Why we do what we do

In 2021 my nephew decided his story didn't need to be written anymore. This was a devastating situation for myself and my family, our normal has changed forever. This situation made me start thinking about my past and even my own son. Unfortunately we have both been where my nephew was. A dark cloud hanging over us that is very hard to crawl from underneath. The stigma society puts on those of us that "are not ok" makes it even harder to reach out for help.

Usually it's easy for me to comfort someone and talk with them and help them through devastating situations in their own lives — when it was time for me to help my own family though it was hard, one of the hardest things I had ever experienced. I reached out blindly not knowing where to turn and found American Foundation for Suicide Prevention.

Since August 2021 my daughter and I have become advocates for AFSP and NAMI and also Overdose Lifeline.

In 2021 with the help of my daughter we founded "Jason's Journey" to keep his story going and to help raise awareness for other people suffering. We quickly realized it wasn't just Jason that had a twizted journey — everyone has a journey and every journey is twizted in its own way. So began Twizted Journeys.

— Tonya Crump, Founder & CEO

Who We Serve

Twizted Journeys provides peer-led support for anyone who has been impacted by suicide loss or mental health challenges. Our events are designed to offer awareness, connection, understanding, prevention, postvention, and a safe space for healing.

Finding Forward — Peer Support Group

  • ✦ Adults 18+
  • ✦ Anyone who has lost a loved one to suicide
  • ✦ Individuals coping with grief, trauma, or emotional stress
  • ✦ People supporting a friend or family member with mental health struggles
  • ✦ Community members seeking coping skills, connection, and resources

Inclusive & Accessible

We welcome all genders, identities, cultures, and socioeconomic backgrounds, including individuals with disabilities or sensory needs.
