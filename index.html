Our Story

Why We Do What We Do

In 2021, Tonya's nephew Jason decided his story didn't need to be written anymore. That loss changed everything — and it made Tonya start thinking about her past, and even her own son. Our family has carried that kind of dark cloud too.

We started "Jason's Journey" to keep his story going and to raise awareness for others who are suffering. We quickly realized — it wasn't just Jason who had a twizted journey. Every journey is twizted in its own way. And so Twizted Journeys began.

Since 2021, we've become advocates with the American Foundation for Suicide Prevention, NAMI, and Overdose Lifeline. We are proud members of the Indiana Suicide Prevention Coalition. We are a 501(c)(3) nonprofit — donations are tax-deductible as allowed by law.

Peer Support Group

Finding Forward

Free · Open to Adults 18+

A Tuesday evening space to heal together

Finding Forward is a peer-led support group for suicide loss survivors and anyone coping with grief, trauma, or emotional stress. This is not a clinical program — it is a community. You don't have to have it together to walk through the door.

📅 Every Tuesday evening
📍 Shelbyville, Indiana
💰 Free, no cost
🤝 All backgrounds welcome

Finding Forward is open to anyone who has lost a loved one to suicide, anyone coping with grief or trauma, people supporting a family member with mental health struggles, and community members seeking connection and coping tools.

Community Stories

Share Your Journey

Your story matters. This is a space to be heard — not judged. Sharing your experience can bring comfort to someone else who is carrying the same weight in silence.

Content Disclosure

Twizted Journeys shares personal stories for awareness, connection, and hope. Some stories may include grief, suicide loss, addiction, overdose, trauma, or mental health struggles. These stories are not therapy, medical advice, crisis counseling, or emergency support. If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call emergency services now. In the United States, call or text 988 or visit 988lifeline.org for crisis support.

If you are under 18: Please have a parent or guardian help you before submitting a story, appearing on the podcast, or sharing personal details publicly. We want your voice heard safely.

Inclusive & Accessible

Who We Welcome

Twizted Journeys is committed to keeping support low-barrier, no-cost, and community-centered. We serve Shelbyville, Indiana and the surrounding counties: Shelby, Johnson, Bartholomew, Hancock, Decatur, and Rush.

  • Adults 18+ who have lost a loved one to suicide
  • Individuals coping with grief, trauma, or emotional stress
  • People supporting a friend or family member with mental health struggles
  • Community members seeking coping skills, connection, and resources
  • Overdose loss survivors
  • Individuals in addiction recovery
  • Mental health advocates and community allies
  • All genders, identities, cultures, and socioeconomic backgrounds
  • Individuals with disabilities or sensory needs
